About CRIMENET

CRIMENET is the first open source knowledge graph of global organized crime. Every node and every relationship is traceable to a versioned Wikipedia source.

Why this exists

Organized crime is global, but our understanding of it is mostly local. It is scattered across news reports, academic papers, and government briefings. There was no single map of who works with whom, who fights whom, and how these groups reach across borders.

CRIMENET fills that gap. It covers cartels, mafias, gangs, motorcycle clubs, triads, clans, factions, militias, and terrorist groups, drawn from nearly 1,500 Wikipedia articles in English, Italian, Portuguese, and Spanish. An LLM pipeline reads every article and extracts every organization mentioned and every alliance or rivalry between them.

The result is a single graph of the global criminal landscape. Every edge carries the verbatim Wikipedia sentence that justifies it, plus a paraphrased description, an extracted time period, and a versioned source URL. Any claim can be audited back to a specific revision of a specific article.

Open source

The code, the dataset, the pipeline, and a detailed technical report are all open source. The GitHub repository has the full pipeline, the audit tools, and the build system that turns the data into this site.
